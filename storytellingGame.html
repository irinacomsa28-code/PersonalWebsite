Serious Game for Social Impact
| bachelor thesis LMU Munich x USI Lugano

What I Did

  • Designed & developed (Unity C#) a serious game to mitigate gender stereotypes in young children
  • Conducted and analyzed two user sessions and iterated based on the collected data
  • Validated game’s effectiveness via data collection a reflection coding scheme

Keywords

  • Child-Computer Interaction
  • Serious Games
  • Reflective Design & Critical Design

Collaborators

  • Prof. Dr. Albrecht Schmidt (LMU Munich)
  • Prof. Dr. Monica Landoni (USI Lugano)
  • PhD student Irene Zanardi (USI Lugano)
  • Primary-school children

For my bachelor thesis, I designed and implemented a serious game for children that encourages reflection on gender stereotypes — a heavily underexplored area within socio-political tools for Child–Computer Interaction (CCI). Developed as part of a collaboration between LMU Munich and USI Lugano (Erasmus university), the project combined academic research with hands-on design and development. The game was designed in Figma, built in Unity (C#) and iteratively refined through user sessions with primary-school children.
